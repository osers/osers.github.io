Home

The initial idea behind OSERS was to be a private in-house rating standard for a variety of projects ranging from video games and anime to movies and tv-shows. That idea was scrapped when it was realized that OSERS could be publicized to act as a primary rating system for open source software, alongside being a solid rating standard for entertainment related purposes and activities, including but not limited to things like anime, cartoons, comics, documentaries, movies, video games, and more.

The OSERS Rating System is fully explained in the "Rating System" tab on the navigation bar. There is also short tests available in the "Receive Rating" tab to easily obtain an OSERS rating for a game or product. Displaying OSERS assets on a product is easy. Simply make sure the product follows the specific rules and guidelines to the respective rating.

What is the purpose behind OSERS? OSERS exists for people who want their products recognizably rated, easily. Accurately self-rate the product using our rating guide or use our rating test to label the product accordingly. This way, the end-user can easily type in the OSERS rating online to see what the product could entail. Without OSERS, the product would likely go unrated, and the end-user would have no rating reference. OSERS was also made for open source games and products that are made available without a traditional storefront.

Is OSERS trying to compete with something like ESRB? No, OSERS and ESRB have two completely different use cases. A developer should always use an IARC (International Age Rating Coalition) member to rate their game, like ESRB in the United States, or PEGI in Europe, if they are publishing to a traditional storefront. Typically, receiving an IARC rating is required by traditional storefronts. OSERS is for developers who are publishing their game or product to something like a personal website. Because they are not using a storefront, receiving an IARC rating could be a headache, or even impossible. Instead, developers can easily use legitimate OSERS ratings.

Quick Rating Reference

[K] Child

0 to 9 years

For Young Children. Cannot contain anything from below.

[N] Neutral

10 years and older

Alcohol Reference, Blood, Cartoon Violence, Comic Mischief, Crude Humor, Fantasy Violence, Tobacco Reference

[W] Warmtemp (formerly Teen [T])

13 years and older

Blood and Gore, Drug Reference, Language, Lyrics, Use of Alcohol, Use of Tobacco, Violence, Violent References

[M] Mature

16 years and older

Intense Violence, Mature Humor, Partial Nudity, Simulated Gambling, Strong Language, Strong Lyrics, Suggestive Themes, Use of Drugs

[X] Adult

18 years and older

Nudity, Real Gambling, Sexual Content, Sexual Themes, Sexual Violence

Accessible to everyone.

Decentralized

OSERS is crowd funded and operated on Open Online Entertainment Forum. Anyone can join and contribute. There is no individual at OOEF that is a part of a solidified central governing body, because it does not exist. Change and action comes in the form of votes, more votes = more change. Change is enacted and accounted by the I.T forum of OOEF.

Operations

OSERS is operated by volunteers who enact and account change. Anyone can request anything, and it is required for it to be voted on. If the vote is successful, the change must be carried out efficiently and effectively by the OSERS Contributors. Become a Contributor.

Compatibility

OSERS is applicable to almost anything entertainment related. Anime, Board Games, Card Games, Cartoons, Comics, Documentaries, Movies, TV Shows, Video Games, and nearly everything else. Perfect for homebrew applications!

M.OSERS will also be available for music rating starting on December 31st, 2021.
